# How to Create a New Job

Status: Not started
Last Updated: January 31, 2026 (PR #12)
Version: 1.1

Transform your hiring process with Fabric's AI-powered recruitment. This
guide walks you through creating compelling job listings that attract
top talent and streamline your screening process.

## Quick Steps

-   Step 1: Upload Job Description\
-   Step 2: Skills and Requirements\
-   Step 3: Conversation Flow\
-   Step 4: Role Information\
-   Step 5: Pre-Interview Questions\
-   Step 6: Launch and Share

Getting started with Fabric is straightforward. Navigate to your Fabric
dashboard and select **Create a New Job** to begin setting up your
interview process.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Step 1: Upload Job Description

Fabric makes it easy to get your job requirements into the system with
two flexible options:

### Option 1: File Upload

Upload your job description in multiple formats including PDF, DOC, or DOCX files directly.

### Option 2: Copy and Paste

Copy your existing job description and paste it into the designated text
field for quick setup.

Once submitted, Fabric's AI processes your job description and
automatically extracts essential details such as required
qualifications, preferred skills, experience levels, and other relevant
criteria.

**Tip:** A well-defined job description is the foundation for success.
Clear, specific requirements help Fabric's AI extract key details
accurately, leading to better candidate matching.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Step 2: Skills and Requirements

This is where you fine-tune what matters most for your role.

## Fundamental Skills

This section displays all core skills an ideal candidate should possess.
Skills can be marked as **Must Have** or **Good to Have**.

### Must Have Skills

Non-negotiable skills required to be effective from day one. Limit this
to your top 5--6 critical skills.

### Good to Have Skills

Secondary skills that strengthen a candidate's profile but are not
mandatory.

## Critical Sections

### Desired Work Experience

Set experience parameters aligned with role complexity and
responsibilities. Useful for specifying industries, company types, and
prior role expectations.

### Education Requirements

-   **College Requirements:** Restrict candidates to specific
    institutions if required.
-   **Degree Requirements:** Specify mandatory degrees.
-   **Certifications:** List professional credentials or licenses.

### Red Flags

Define patterns that indicate potential concerns, such as frequent job
changes, undesirable background profiles, or missing critical skills.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Step 3: Conversation Flow

Configure your AI Interview Assistant and the questions it will ask
candidates.

## Interview Format Selection

Choose from three interview format options to match your hiring stage:

- **Resume Discussion:** Focus on the candidate's background and experience
- **Screening Interview:** Initial evaluation of qualifications and fit
- **Round 1 Interview:** Comprehensive assessment for advancing candidates

## Quick Select Assistants

Choose an AI personality for interviews. Fabric supports multiple
languages and accents. The system will automatically switch to Fabric Alpha for advanced questions such as coding assessments when needed.

Popular examples: - Anushka and Shubham for Indian English\
- Rachel for US English\
- Paula for Australian English

## Resume-based Questions

Dynamic questions generated from a candidate's resume. You can set focus
areas like education or work experience and add additional instructions.

## Must Ask Questions

Fabric auto-generates questions based on your job description and
must-have skills. These ensure consistent evaluation across candidates.

### Question Framework

Each question includes: - Question - Difficulty level - Skills being
tested - Number of follow-ups - Purpose - Follow-up directions -
Expected answer benchmarks

### Customization Options

-   Regenerate questions
-   Add or delete questions
-   Edit questions and auto-generate supporting fields

## Additional Sections

### Logistic Questions

Covers availability, notice period, and salary expectations. These are
stored for review and not used for evaluation.

### Guesstimate Questions

Analytical problem-solving questions from a question bank, with the
option to add custom ones.

### Puzzle Questions

Creative problem-solving questions from a question bank, with custom
options available.

**Tip:** A good combination is 1 resume question, 3 must-ask questions,
and 1 guesstimate question.

## Assistant Details and Settings

-   Agent name
-   Company name phonetics
-   Role name phonetics
-   Custom start and end instructions
-   Next steps communication instructions

# Step 4: Role Information

Define essential job details visible to candidates (except salary
range).

-   Role name
-   Job summary
-   Seniority level
-   Location
-   Employment type
-   Workplace type

## Eligibility Toggles

### Years of Experience

Automatically reject candidates below minimum experience.

### Salary Range

Automatically reject candidates above maximum salary expectations.
Salary range is not visible to candidates.

### Resume Requirements

Control whether candidates must submit a resume and whether resume screening is enabled. These settings can be configured independently:

- **Require Resume:** Make resume submission mandatory for applications
- **Resume Screening:** Enable AI-powered resume screening and evaluation

### Interview Settings

- **Interview Enabled:** Toggle to enable or disable interviews for this position
- **Interview Reschedule Limit:** Set the maximum number of times candidates can reschedule their interviews

### Responsibilities

Define core duties and responsibilities of the role.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Step 5: Pre-Interview Questions

Add form-based questions to learn more about candidates.

Question types: - MCQ - Yes/No - Number - Free text

Pre-interview questions can also be used as eligibility filters, such
as: - Joining timeline - Relocation willingness - Portfolio link

## Application Form Customization

Control which information candidates provide during application. You can hide the entire application form or specific fields as needed to streamline your candidate experience.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Step 6: Launch and Share

Finish your job listing to generate a shareable link.

You can: - Post on job portals - Share directly with candidates - Bulk
upload resumes for screening and AI interviews - Import multiple candidates at once using CSV file upload

## Bulk Candidate Upload

Use the CSV upload feature to import multiple candidates efficiently. This allows you to quickly add candidate information in bulk for screening and interview processing.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Wrapping Up

Your job role and AI Interview assistant are now created.

Fabric evaluates candidates using a scoring framework out of 10.
